Dashboards can serve a larger role than simple data display because they help organizations preserve, disseminate, and act on knowledge that would otherwise remain fragmented or be lost through turnover. Banerjee et al. (2025) argue that knowledge extraction and sharing are major organizational challenges and show that web-based Tableau dashboards can support evidence-driven decision-making, long-term knowledge repositories, and organizational innovation. Their discussion is especially relevant to this project because it connects dashboards with the institutionalization of knowledge, the need to support end users effectively, and the risk that poor knowledge management can lead to financial loss and reduced productivity. In that sense, dashboards should be understood not only as reporting tools, but also as mechanisms for maintaining organizational memory and supporting decision continuity across time, personnel changes, and operational complexity (Banerjee et al., 2025).

In the context of this project, Banerjee et al. (2025) help justify the argument that Tableau reports at Aera Energy were not merely visual interfaces, but repositories of operational and institutional knowledge. If dashboards can function as tools for knowledge dissemination, decision support, and organizational continuity, then the migration of Tableau reports to Spotfire becomes more than a software replacement task. It becomes a knowledge-preservation challenge. This is especially important in an environment shaped by personnel turnover, evolving operational practices, and the need to retain access to business-critical logic embedded in dashboards. From that perspective, the loss of Tableau access without a structured extraction process would risk losing not only visualizations, but also the accumulated analytical meaning that supports consistent decision-making across the enterprise (Banerjee et al., 2025).
