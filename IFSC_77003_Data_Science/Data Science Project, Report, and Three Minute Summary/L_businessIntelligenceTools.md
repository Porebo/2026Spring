Business Intelligence tools play a central role in helping organizations collect, organize, integrate, and analyze data for managerial decision-making. Zierz and Lordsleem Junior (2026) describe BI as a set of methods and technologies that transform data from multiple sources into dashboards, dynamic reports, and more accessible analytical views for organizational use. Their review highlights tools such as Power BI, Tableau, and Qlik as major platforms for integrating spreadsheets, databases, management systems, and cloud-based data into usable reporting environments. This is important because it confirms that dashboards are part of a larger BI ecosystem in which value comes not only from visualization, but also from the integration of diverse data sources into forms that support monitoring, efficiency, and decision-making (Zierz & Lordsleem Junior, 2026).

In the context of this project, Zierz and Lordsleem Junior (2026) help situate Tableau and Spotfire within the broader category of Business Intelligence tools used to turn enterprise data into practical decision-support systems. At Aera Energy, Tableau functioned as one such BI layer on top of curated warehouse data, making operational information from multiple source systems accessible to engineers, geologists, technicians, and managers. Their discussion of challenges such as data standardization, interoperability, infrastructure, and user resistance is also relevant to the Aera-to-CRC transition, where technical compatibility and user familiarity shaped the difficulty of moving from Tableau to Spotfire. This supports the argument that BI migration is not only a visual redesign task, but an organizational and technical transition within a larger data integration environment (Zierz & Lordsleem Junior, 2026).
