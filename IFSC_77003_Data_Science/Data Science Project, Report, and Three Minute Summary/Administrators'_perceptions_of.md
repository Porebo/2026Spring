Dashboards provide practical value within organizations because they centralize important information in a form that supports timely interpretation, monitoring, and action. In a study of administrators' perceptions of dashboard implementation in a Texas charter school system, Caglar (2016) found that dashboards were valued as a "one-stop shop" for accessing data, helping users monitor progress, share information, and make more effective data-informed decisions. Although the study is situated in an educational setting, its relevance to this project lies in its organizational perspective: dashboards are not useful only because they display metrics, but because they create a centralized, usable environment where decision-makers can quickly reach the information they need. This supports the broader argument that dashboards should be understood as practical decision-support tools embedded in organizational workflow, rather than as passive reporting screens alone (Caglar, 2016).

In the context of this project, Caglar's (2016) findings help explain why Tableau dashboards at Aera Energy became so important to everyday operations. If dashboards function as a centralized environment where users access information quickly, monitor performance, and support decision-making, then the Tableau reports used across Aera were not simply technical outputs. They were practical working tools embedded in the daily workflow of engineers, geologists, technicians, and managers. This strengthens the argument that migrating from Tableau to Spotfire is not only a matter of recreating visuals, but also of preserving the practical decision-support role that those dashboards played across the organization. Losing access to that dashboard environment without adequately preserving its underlying logic and structure would risk disrupting how professionals interact with data in real operational settings (Caglar, 2016).
