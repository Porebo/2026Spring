# Model 1: Housing Affordability Visualizations

**Page Range:** 9-19 of 28

## VISUALIZATIONS

### FIGURE 1: MEDIAN HOUSEHOLD INCOME (LINE CHART)

This line chart displays year-over-year changes in median household income for selected cities, making it easy to observe long-term income trends and compare cities directly.

### FIGURE 2: MEDIAN HOUSEHOLD INCOME VS. MEDIAN HOME PRICE (SCATTER PLOT)

This scatter plot compares median household income to median home price across U.S. cities for the most recent year available. Each point represents a city, allowing viewers to quickly see the relationship between income and home price at the local level. The scatter plot helps identify cities where home prices are disproportionately high relative to income, highlighting affordability challenges. Outliers, where home prices far exceed what local incomes could support, are easily spotted using this format.

### FIGURE 3: MEDIAN GROSS RENT (LINE CHART)

This line chart tracks median gross rent for selected U.S. cities from 2000 to 2024. The line chart format effectively illustrates year-over-year changes in rental costs, helping to visualize how quickly rents have increased in certain cities. This visualization also makes it easy to compare rental price trends across multiple cities, highlighting where rental affordability may have worsened due to faster-than-average rent increases.

### FIGURE 4: POPULATION (LINE CHART)

This line chart displays population trends for selected U.S. cities from 2000 to 2024. The line chart format helps highlight long-term growth or decline, allowing for easy comparisons between cities. Tracking population changes is critical for understanding demand-side pressures on housing markets, as cities experiencing rapid population growth often face rising housing costs and affordability challenges.

### FIGURE 5: MEDIAN HOME COST (LINE CHART)

This line chart tracks median home prices for selected U.S. cities from 2000 to 2024. The line format highlights the pace and scale of home price changes over time, making it easy to spot periods of rapid price appreciation or market corrections. By comparing trends across cities, this visualization helps identify which areas have seen the steepest increases in home costs, contributing to affordability challenges.

### FIGURE 6: INCOME VS RENT PRICE (RENT AFFORDABILITY) (SCATTER PLOT)

This scatter plot compares median household income to annual gross rent for cities across the United States. Each point represents a city, allowing for a visual comparison of how well local incomes cover rental costs. Cities positioned closer to the diagonal trend line show more balanced rent affordability, where incomes align closely with rents. Outliers, where rents far exceed incomes, can be easily identified, highlighting cities with severe rental affordability challenges. This visualization helps emphasize where rent burdens are disproportionately high, particularly in areas with stagnant income growth or rising housing demand.

### FIGURE 7: HOME AFFORDABILITY (HEAT MAP)

This heat map displays home affordability ratios across selected cities, with each city color-coded to reflect its relative affordability. Cities with higher affordability ratios (more affordable) appear in cooler colors (green), while less affordable cities appear in warmer colors (red). Grouped by state, this visualization helps identify regional patterns in home affordability, making it easy to spot clusters of affordable or unaffordable areas.

### FIGURE 8: RENT AFFORDABILITY (HEAT MAP)

This heat map visualizes rent affordability ratios across selected U.S. cities, with color coding to indicate relative affordability. Cities where rental costs consume a smaller portion of income are shown in cooler colors (green), while cities with high rent burdens are shown in warmer colors (red). Grouped by state, this visualization helps uncover regional patterns, highlighting areas where rent affordability is particularly strained.

### FIGURE 9: TOP 30 MOST AFFORDABLE CITIES (HOUSING) (BAR CHART)

This horizontal bar chart ranks the most affordable cities for homeownership, based on the affordability ratio (median household income divided by median home price) for the most recent year. Horizontal bars allow for easy comparison across cities, with longer bars indicating greater affordability. This visualization highlights the cities where homeownership remains most accessible relative to local incomes.

### FIGURE 10: TOP 30 MOST AFFORDABLE CITIES (RENT) (BAR CHART)

This horizontal bar chart ranks the most affordable cities for renters, based on the rent affordability ratio (median household income divided by annual gross rent) for the most recent year. Longer bars indicate greater rental affordability, where median income comfortably covers housing costs. This visualization helps identify cities where rental housing remains attainable for typical households.

### FIGURE 11: TOP 30 LEAST AFFORDABLE CITIES (HOUSING) (BAR CHART)

This horizontal bar chart ranks the least affordable cities for homeownership, based on the affordability ratio (median household income divided by median home price) for the most recent year. Shorter bars indicate lower affordability, meaning local home prices far exceed typical household incomes. This visualization highlights cities where housing affordability is most strained, particularly in high-cost metropolitan areas.
