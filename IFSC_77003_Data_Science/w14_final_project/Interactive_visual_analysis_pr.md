Interactive visual analysis does more than display data; it supports exploration, sense-making, and hypothesis generation in ways that static charts and spreadsheets often cannot. Pham et al. (2013) argue that interactive visualization enables users to iteratively explore complex, multivariate, and long-term data before committing to formal statistical analysis. Their work shows that interactivity, ease of use, holistic access to large data sets, and user-centered design can help professionals move beyond simple observation toward active knowledge generation. This is important because it positions visual analysis tools not as passive reporting media, but as environments that support analytical thinking and discovery (Pham et al., 2013).

In the context of this project, Pham et al. (2013) help explain why Tableau dashboards at Aera Energy became so valuable to professional workers. The reports were not merely static displays of curated warehouse data; they functioned as interactive environments where engineers, geologists, technicians, and managers could filter, compare, and explore operational information in order to notice patterns and support decisions. This strengthens the argument that the value of Tableau was not limited to code, formulas, and visual layout alone, but also included the interactive analytical experience it provided. As a result, migration from Tableau to Spotfire must preserve not only data logic and visual structure, but also the exploratory and sense-making capabilities through which users turned data into practical knowledge (Pham et al., 2013).
