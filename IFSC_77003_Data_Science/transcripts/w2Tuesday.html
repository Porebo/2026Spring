The Narrative of Data

This session humanized the technical reporting process by framing a Data Science project as a **story**. Dr. Pierce's "movie analogy"—setting the stage and developing characters (questions)—is a brilliant way to make documentation engaging. The lecture also displayed high empathy for privacy constraints, suggesting sophisticated workarounds like data simulation when real data is locked behind regulations like FERPA.

Quick Summary

The lecture focused on "project shopping" (finding ideas and data), navigating Data Use Agreements (DUA), and the rigorous "50% effort" phase of Data Cleaning. Students were encouraged to explore diverse datasets from local hubs to global APIs (YouTube, Netflix) and learned recipes for handling missingness, duplicates, and outliers.

🎬 The Movie Analogy: Telling your Data Story

A good data report is not a dry log; it’s a narrative:

  • Set the Stage: Define the context (e.g., Old West vs. Future). Why does this problem matter?
  • Develop the Characters: Your characters are your Questions. Show their motivations and why investigating them is worthwhile.

🛒 "Project Shopping"

Don't have an idea? Start with the data! Explore:

  • Kaggle/Challenges: See what others are solving.
  • Little Rock Data Hub: Local stats on crime, parks, and zoo admissions.
  • Gov/Global: Data.gov, World Bank, UNICEF, and US Census.

🌐 APIs & Unstructured Data

Push the envelope beyond spreadsheets:

  • Media: YouTube, Netflix, and Music APIs.
  • Text: Project Gutenberg (75k books).
  • IoT: Live sensor feeds and webcam images.

🧹 The 50% Rule: Data Cleaning Recipes

Data is rarely perfect. Common "wrangling" tasks include:

  • Missing Data: Decide to remove or impute (fill with mean/median/best guess).
  • Duplicates: Look for "Elizabeth Pierce" vs. "Liz Pierce" inconsistencies.
  • Normalization: Standardize units (kg vs lbs) and formats (01/22/26 vs Jan 22).
  • Outliers: Use Box Plots to find values more than 1.5x the Interquartile Range (IQR).
  • Integrity: Check for "Data Entry Errors" (e.g., birth dates in the future).

⚖️ Data Use Agreements (DUA)

Understand the rules of the data you find:

  • Education: Most APIs allow free "fair use" for learning.
  • Commercial: Licensing is required if you plan to sell an app using the data.
  • Acknowledgement: Always cite your source!

🧪 Privacy Workaround

If data is protected (like student FERPA records) or certain conditions are rare, use Mockaroo to simulate realistic datasets for testing your models.

Professional Perspective

Info Science vs. Info Quality

Dr. Pierce explains that our department is split into Analysis (Science) and Fitness-for-use (Quality). A great data scientist must master both: you can't have reliable insights without "clean" data.

Career Advice

Domain Knowledge Matters

Pick a project area where you have "subject matter expertise." It’s hard to tell a story about the Old West if you've never seen a horse.

The "Team" Approach: In industry, data science is a team sport. One person knows stats, one knows the domain, and one knows the tech. Together, they find the "Bun & Hot Dog" associations that matter.
