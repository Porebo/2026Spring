🔢 The 4 Traditional Data Types

The foundation of structured data analysis:

  1. Nominal: Labels (Gender, Manufacturer).
  2. Ordinal: Ordered categories (Class ranking, Satisfaction).
  3. Discrete: Countable whole numbers (Number of cars).
  4. Continuous: Measured values with decimals (Weight, Length).

📈 Data Evolution

20% Structured: Traditional database data.

80% Unstructured: Video, audio, DNA, sensors, and social media.

Data Analyst: Focuses on historical/past data.

Data Scientist: Forward-looking, future predictions, and ML models.

🔄 The Project Pipeline

1. Discovery: Finding the question (50% of the work).
2. Data Collection: Use existing datasets (Kaggle/data.gov) to save time.
3. Modeling: Start simple (Regression) before going complex.
4. Fine-tuning: Optimizing for accuracy.
5. Operationalizing: Putting models into production (e.g., Target pregnancy alerts or Kroger inventory).

The "Walmart" Success Story

A former student leveraged his passion for college football to bridge the gap into professional analysis. Instead of just "wins and losses," he analyzed recruiting efficacy and yardage patterns.

Key Insight: You don't need a "sledgehammer" (complex ML) if a "small hammer" (basic stats) solves the problem thoughtfully. This project helped him land a job as an analyst at Walmart.
