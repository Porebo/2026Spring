Lecture Summary: Course Overview & Introduction

Week 1 | Jan 20, 2026 | Session 1

1. Course Philosophy & Structure

Dr. Liz Pierce introduced the course as a "sampler" or "buffet" of data science topics, designed for students of all backgrounds.

  • Goal: Provide a strong foundation where students can "learn how to think" and gain insights from data.
  • Customization: Students are encouraged to tailor their learning to their specific career interests (e.g., healthcare, finance, sports).
  • Portfolio-Based: The ultimate goal is to create a project portfolio that can be shown to employers.

2. Grading & Requirements

  • Weekly Assignments (50%): Small tasks to reinforce the theme of the week.
  • Data Science Project (50%): The main core of the course. Custom-chosen by the student to solve a meaningful problem.
  • Attendance: Tuesday/Thursday (1:40 - 2:55 PM). Sessions are recorded; asynchronous attendance is supported.

3. Tools & Resources

"This course does not have a textbook... I wanted to make sure students did not have to pay any additional out-of-pocket costs."

  • Resources: Using Open Educational Resources (OER) for Statistics, Databases, Data Mining, and AI.
  • Primary Tool: Python using Google Colab (cloud-based Jupyter Notebooks).
  • Other Options: R, Weka, SAS, SPSS, and Tableau are acceptable for projects if appropriate.

4. AI as a "Thought Partner"

The course introduces the use of AI as a "Co-pilot."

  • AI is useful for brainstorming, code scripts, and exploring insights.
  • The Rule: You must stay in the "driver's seat." Do not let AI call the shots; you must be able to trust your own instincts and fact-check the output.

5. Semester Roadmap (The Sampler)

The course will cover a wide range of data types and technologies:

  • Structured Data: Spreadsheets, rows, and columns.
  • Text Analytics: Unstructured text, sentiment analysis, and themes.
  • Multimedia & Images: Video, sound, and computer vision (facial recognition).
  • IoT & Streaming Data: Real-time data from sensors (Ring doorbells, medical devices).
  • Social Media Analytics: Mention of the UALR COSMOS research group.
  • Emerging Tech: Cloud computing, Bio-informatics, and Chem-informatics.

6. Next Steps

  • Identify potential project ideas and data sources.
  • Familiarize yourself with the Blackboard course management system.
  • Watch for upcoming weekly assignments and weather announcements.

7. AI Assistant's Impressions

Key takeaways on the course's "vibe" and approach:

  • Student-Centric & Practical: Emphasis on a "project portfolio" over rote testing to help with career advancement.
  • Highly Accessible: A "buffet" approach that welcomes all backgrounds and provides flexibility for advanced students.
  • Forward-Thinking: AI is treated as a "thought partner" rather than a prohibited tool, reflecting modern workplace reality.
  • Cost-Conscious: Strong commitment to zero out-of-pocket costs by using only free resources.
  • Broad Horizons: Exposure to diverse fields like IoT, Bio-informatics, and Text Analytics provides a solid foundation for specialization.
