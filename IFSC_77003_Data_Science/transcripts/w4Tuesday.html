AI Impressions

The Evolution of Text Analytics

This session officially transitioned the course into Unstructured Data Analysis. Dr. Pierce highlighted a critical shift: while structured data is easy to manage, it only represents a small fraction of the world's information. The move toward Text Analytics marks the transition from purely numerical computation to understanding human language at scale.

Quick Summary

The lecture provided a recap of structured data analysis and introduced the "unstructured reality" of text. We explored the nuances between Text Mining and Text Analytics, covered essential pre-processing techniques (tokenization, stemming, lemmatization), and looked at real-world applications across healthcare, finance, and legal sectors.

📊 Recap: Structured Data Analysis

A look back at what we've covered in the first three weeks:

  • Diabetes Prevention: Using biomedical indicators to predict risk.
  • Data Cleaning: Handling astronomical outliers and missing values.
  • Decision Trees: Visualizing logic for complex predictions.

🌍 The Unstructured Reality

Why text matters:

  • Structured data is only 20-30% of available data.
  • The majority is unstructured: images, sound, DNA, and text.

🔍 Mining vs. Analytics

A subtle but important difference:

  • Text Mining: Focuses on extraction.
  • Text Analytics: Focuses on deriving insights.

🧹 Text Pre-processing Techniques

The "cleaning" phase for language data:

  • Tokenization: Breaking text into words.
  • Stop Words: Removing low-value words (a, the, and).
  • Stemming/Lemmatization: Finding the base form of words.
  • NER: Identifying names, places, and organizations.

🚀 Major Applications in 2025

  • Healthcare: Clinical notes + records = better predictions.
  • Finance: Fraud detection in transaction logs.
  • Sentiment: Social media mood tracking.
  • Legal: Smart contract and case file searching.

⚠️ AI & Hallucinations

While LLMs (ChatGPT, Claude) have revolutionized this field, they are not infallible:

"LLMs can still 'hallucinate' facts... Human fact-checking remains critical for academic research and high-stakes summaries."

🛠️ Technical Tools

  • NLTK & spaCy: Python text analysis.
  • Regex: Pattern matching for text.
  • Ngram Viewer: Low-code word frequency analysis.
