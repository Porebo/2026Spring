Task 2: Quantitative Website Evaluation

University: Universidad del Valle de Guatemala (UVG)

Dimension: Credibility (Method 2: Breadcrumb Structure Analysis)

Quantitative Score: 0%

Methodology

This evaluation utilized the "Information Quality" framework discussed in the Jan 27 lecture. Specifically, I performed a structural audit of 5 core navigational hubs on the UVG domain to check for the presence of hierarchical breadcrumb trails.

  • Admissions: No breadcrumbs found.
  • Academic Faculties: No breadcrumbs found.
  • Student Wellness: No breadcrumbs found.
  • Research Centers: No breadcrumbs found.
  • Campus Information: No breadcrumbs found.

Analysis

The 0% score highlights UVG's modern "flat" design philosophy. While the site is highly professional and visually engaging, the lack of visible navigational paths makes it difficult for external users to verify the institutional hierarchy of a page at a glance. This can impact the Authoritativeness component of credibility for users arriving from external search engines.

Critique of Method

Testing for breadcrumbs is a strong measure of structural transparency, but it is a flawed measure of overall credibility. Modern web design often replaces text-based trails with "Mega Menus" or sticky navigation to improve experience on mobile devices. This metric penalizes innovation and rewards "old-school" library-style architectures, regardless of whether the content itself is believable or accurate.
