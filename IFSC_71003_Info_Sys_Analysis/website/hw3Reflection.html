Part 5: Reflection

Two short reflections: (A) HW2 improvements & keeps, (B) Socratic AI experience and next prompting strategy.

Reflection A — HW2: Improve / Keep

Exploring the bulletin boards was a useful learning adventure because I don't normally use them. Although many people rely on these boards, I learned new information and give them a keep score.

The quantitative website activities helped me evaluate site quality and introduced me to breadcrumb navigation, which I now use consistently on my own site — this also receives a keep score.

Initially I gave HTML tags a "lose" score because AI handles much of the hands-on coding and the mechanics can seem less essential. It's like owning a car: you don't need to know how it works, just how to drive it. After reflecting, I changed my mind — basic coding awareness remains valuable, so I now give HTML tags a keep score.

Reflection B — Socratic AI Experience

Socratic questioning is a high-level metacognitive activity; I give it a keep score. It encourages internal reflection and effectively stretches my thinking. Going forward, I'll continue using Socratic prompts to probe assumptions and identify actionable next steps.
