Why the 50% Minimum is "Based" (Logic & Opinion)

In my opinion, the 50-100 scale is a much more accurate way to measure learning. When the scale is 0-100, the "math isn't mathing" because the failing range (0-60) is six times larger than the range for an A (90-100). This creates a mathematical black hole.

Key Takeaways:

  • Balancing the Meta: By setting the floor at 50%, an "F" is now only 10 points wide (50-60), making it equal to an A, B, or C.
  • No More Griefing: On a 0-100 scale, a single zero can "grief" your entire GPA. Even with perfect scores elsewhere, a zero acts as a massive anchor. The 50% rule prevents one bad week from ruining a whole semester.
  • The Redemption Arc: This system allows for a comeback. It prioritizes what you actually learned by the end of the course rather than just punishing you for early mistakes.
Personal Experience with Grading

I definitely agree with the 50-100 scale because I have seen the "Zero Problem" happen IRL. In almost every class I have taken, the 0-100 scale was the standard. I've experienced the frustration of missing just one homework assignment and receiving a 0, which immediately pulled down all my other hard-earned grades. It feels like the math is working against you rather than measuring your actual effort.

A Different Approach:

I once had a class that tried to accommodate this by dropping the lowest score within certain categories. While that was helpful, I think the 50-100 scale is even better because it addresses the root of the problem: the disproportionate weight of the failing range. The 50% floor ensures that every assignment—even a failed one—remains within a range that allows for a successful recovery.

Conclusion: The 50-100 system is much more "based" because it values the learning process over mathematical punishment.
