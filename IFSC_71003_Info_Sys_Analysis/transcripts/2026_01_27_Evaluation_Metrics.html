Lecture Summary: Website Evaluation Quality Metrics

Module 1 | Jan 27, 2026 | 04:24 PM

1. Narrative of Activities

In this session, we explored the concept of Quantitative Evaluation for information systems, specifically focusing on websites. The lecture used university websites as a primary case study to demonstrate how subjective "quality" can be measured using objective, numerical data. We walked through the calculation of various metrics and discussed how different design philosophies (modern "flat" vs. traditional hierarchical) affect these scores.

2. The 6 Dimensions of Website Quality

The core of the lecture was the Information Quality Framework, which breaks down site evaluation into six key dimensions:

DimensionHow It's Measured
UsefulnessRatio of successful sessions to total sessions
UsabilityRatio of successful instructional tasks vs. help requests
FindabilityPercentage of time a site appears in Top 10 search results for relevant queries
AccessibilityCompliance with scanners or percentage of images containing alt text
CredibilityPresence of clear site structure (e.g., Breadcrumbs)
ValuePercentage of essential materials available without password protection

3. Key Insights

Be critical of the evaluation methods. A "perfect" score in one dimension might not mean the website provides the best user experience (e.g., Breadcrumbs vs. Mega Menus).

  • Modern "flat" designs often score 0% on Credibility despite being professional — this highlights a flaw in the metric.
  • The framework provides objective numbers for what is typically a subjective judgment.
  • Different design philosophies result in very different scores across dimensions.
