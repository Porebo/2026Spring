Skills Snapshot

My academic foundation spans two decades of continuous higher education across five institutions, reflecting a deliberate and sustained commitment to intellectual growth, technical mastery, and interdisciplinary scholarship. My formal education began at Lompoc High School, Lompoc, California, where I earned my diploma in 1996 — a starting point that would eventually lead to one of the most expansive and diverse academic journeys in the fields of data science and geoscience.

I hold a Bachelor of Science from California State University, Bakersfield (2009), with a triple concentration in Psychology, Religious Studies, and Geology — a multidisciplinary combination that established strong analytical reasoning, earth science intuition, and behavioral understanding foundational to data-driven decision making in complex scientific environments. Most recently, I completed a Bachelor of Arts in Interdisciplinary Studies from the University of Arkansas at Little Rock (2025), with concentrations in Academic Research Methods, Technical Writing, and Information Technology. This degree, complemented by an undergraduate Certificate in Information Technology with specialization in SQL and Database Architecture, represents the convergence of my scientific literacy, communication expertise, and technical fluency into a unified academic credential — directly applicable to the research, documentation, and data systems demands of advanced professional and graduate-level work.

Complementing my baccalaureate education, I earned nine associate degrees and certificates from Bakersfield College (2016–2019), spanning English, Economics, History, Liberal Arts and Sciences, and a California State University General Education Breadth Certificate of Achievement — collectively reinforcing my command of quantitative reasoning, critical thinking, written communication, and cross-disciplinary analysis. My earlier academic work at Allan Hancock College (2006) produced two Associate in Arts degrees in Liberal Arts and Transfer Studies, establishing the communication and compositional competencies that underpin my technical documentation and data storytelling capabilities.

My transition into information technology and data science was formalized through Cerro Coso Community College (2019–2020), where I earned two Associate in Science degrees and five Certificates of Achievement in Business Office Technology, Administrative Office Systems, CC Information Technology, Information Technology Plus, and Data Analyst I — with a cumulative institutional GPA of 3.64. This phase of my academic career directly established my fluency in database architecture, project management, office systems integration, and computational problem solving.

I am currently completing a Graduate Certificate in Data Science at the University of Arkansas at Little Rock (UALR), with plans to complete a Master of Science in Information Technology in 2026, followed by application to a doctoral program in Computer Science. This graduate trajectory reflects a deliberate and strategic progression toward research-level expertise in data systems, machine learning, and computational intelligence — directly aligned with the demands of modern subsurface data science in the energy sector.

Professional Experience

My academic development has run in parallel with over 17 years of applied professional experience in the oil and gas industry. From 2008 to 2025, I served as a Geotechnical Specialist at Aera Energy LLC, one of California's largest oil and gas producers operating in the San Joaquin Valley. In this role, I applied my combined expertise in geoscience, data management, and information technology to support subsurface data analysis, interpretation workflows, and enterprise data systems. This long-term industry engagement provided the applied context in which my academic training in geology, economics, database systems, Oracle SQL, and data visualization converged into a coherent and high-impact professional skill set. My work at Aera Energy directly informed my proficiency in oil and gas data warehouse (ODW) environments and my advanced use of TIBCO Spotfire for subsurface analytical dashboard development.

Technical Proficiencies

My programming and data visualization competencies span multiple languages and platforms developed through formal academic coursework and extensive professional application. I am proficient and experienced in Python, Java, C++, C#, BASIC, MATLAB, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — providing full-stack awareness across scripting, object-oriented development, numerical computing, and web technologies. In the domain of data visualization and business intelligence, I bring hands-on professional experience with both Tableau and TIBCO Spotfire, with advanced working knowledge of Spotfire version 14.6.0 including dimension set architecture and analytical dashboard development. My database expertise includes Oracle SQL with applied experience in oil and gas data warehouse (ODW) environments, where I design, optimize, and maintain complex queries supporting subsurface data analysis and reporting workflows. This specialization is formally recognized through my undergraduate Certificate in Information Technology with a concentration in SQL and Database Architecture from UALR, reflecting both academic preparation and sustained professional application in enterprise data environments.

This combination of formal academic depth, continuous self-directed learning, over 17 years of industry experience, and an active graduate research trajectory positions me as a uniquely qualified candidate at the intersection of data science, information technology, and geoscience — capable of bridging the gap between raw subsurface data and actionable analytical intelligence.
