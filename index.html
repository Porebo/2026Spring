Professional Experience

My academic development has run in parallel with over 17 years of applied professional experience in the oil and gas industry. From 2008 to 2025, I served as a Geotechnical Specialist at Aera Energy LLC, one of California's largest oil and gas producers operating in the San Joaquin Valley. In this role, I applied my combined expertise in geoscience, data management, and information technology to support subsurface data analysis, interpretation workflows, and enterprise data systems. This long-term industry engagement provided the applied context in which my academic training in geology, economics, database systems, Oracle SQL, and data visualization converged into a coherent and high-impact professional skill set. My work at Aera Energy directly informed my proficiency in oil and gas data warehouse (ODW) environments and my advanced use of TIBCO Spotfire for subsurface analytical dashboard development.

In 2025, California Resources Corporation (CRC) completed its acquisition of Aera Energy LLC, and I transitioned into my current role as a Subsurface Data Specialist with CRC — one of California's leading independent oil and gas exploration and production companies. This transition was a natural continuation of my professional trajectory, as CRC's acquisition brought forward the same subsurface data infrastructure, enterprise systems, and analytical workflows I had developed and maintained throughout my tenure at Aera.

In my current capacity as Subsurface Data Specialist, I serve as the lead technical contributor on a critical data visualization modernization initiative focused on migrating the organization's geology data management dashboards from Tableau to TIBCO Spotfire. While my years at Aera Energy had established a strong foundation in Tableau development, Spotfire presented an entirely new challenge — its underlying mechanics, logic architecture, and workflow paradigms differ substantially from Tableau, requiring me to approach the platform as a genuine beginner and build proficiency from the ground up. This has been a demanding but deeply rewarding discovery learning curve, one in which my interdisciplinary academic training — spanning data systems, information science, business intelligence, and computational reasoning — has proven instrumental in accelerating my command of the platform and enabling me to succeed as the project's technical lead. Though I operate as an individual contributor rather than a manager or supervisor, this experience stands as a compelling demonstration of how sustained academic investment and applied professional experience can converge to meet entirely new technical challenges with confidence and capability.
